# Source Assumptions

The project uses the uploaded CSV, Excel, script, DOCX, and PDF sources as the analytical foundation. The following assumptions were carried into the portfolio project.

## Product and Pricing

- Product category: handmade scented candles and home fragrance.
- Positioning: giftable, handcrafted, eco-friendly, and lifestyle-oriented.
- Initial production cost: INR 129.70 per candle.
- Selling price: INR 195 per candle.
- Pricing logic: approximately 1.5x production cost.
- Break-even estimate: about 30 candles per 50-candle production batch.

## Cost Structure

The original business context included raw materials, labor, and overhead:

- Wax, fragrance oils, dye, containers, labels, and packaging.
- Semi-skilled production labor when hired.
- Electricity, LPG, and home-based overhead.

The advanced model assumes a 15% unit cost reduction beginning in October 2025, moving unit cost from INR 129.70 to about INR 110.25.

## Demand and Operations

- Demand is affected by weekends, gifting behavior, festivals, customer preference shifts, and seasonality.
- Diwali-period demand is modeled through a 50% uplift around 2025-10-20 and 2025-11-01.
- The advanced scenario models a production capacity ceiling of 130 units per day.
- Actual daily units peaked at 100 in the provided actual data.

## Risk Assumptions

- Market volatility can move demand around the expected trend.
- Procurement or delivery disruptions can reduce sales capacity.
- The stress-test layer assumes a 5% delay probability and a 30% unit reduction on delayed days.

## Interpretation Note

The advanced model is interpreted as a planning and stress-test model. It is useful for understanding operational exposure, but actual-vs-forecast results show that it should be calibrated monthly with observed demand and capacity performance.
