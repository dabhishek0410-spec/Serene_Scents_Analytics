# Executive Summary

Serene Scents was an early-stage scented candle venture focused on handmade home fragrance and giftable products. From July 2025 to January 2026, the business generated INR 1,996,800 in revenue, INR 834,328 in profit, and sold 10,240 units. The strongest operating story was not only revenue growth, but the improvement in unit economics after cost optimization.

The business moved from a 33.5% gross margin before October to 43.5% afterward. This was driven by a modeled 15% unit cost reduction, from INR 129.70 to about INR 110.25, while holding the selling price at INR 195. In practical terms, procurement maturity created profit leverage without requiring a price increase.

Demand was highly seasonal. Festival windows produced a 54.2% higher average daily revenue than non-festival periods, and weekend demand was 61.6% higher than weekday demand. December was the strongest month at INR 505,830 revenue, but January fell 34.1%, showing that the founder needed to separate temporary seasonal lift from recurring baseline demand.

The forecasting journey evolved in two phases. The basic model provided a quick financial estimate using simple unit growth, pricing, and cost assumptions. The advanced model introduced weekly and monthly seasonality, Diwali boosts, market volatility, procurement delay impact, and capacity constraints. The advanced scenario overestimated actual revenue by 50.2%, which became a useful learning point: richer operating logic must be calibrated continuously against real actuals.

Strategically, the analysis supports five founder decisions:

- Plan inventory and packaging ahead of gift-heavy festival windows.
- Negotiate raw material procurement earlier to protect gross margin.
- Monitor daily capacity against weekend and festival demand spikes.
- Use scenario forecasting for operational readiness, not just revenue optimism.
- Build actual-vs-forecast review loops to recalibrate assumptions monthly.

The project is designed for a LinkedIn portfolio, GitHub showcase, and analyst interview discussion because it connects technical modeling to business judgment, operating constraints, and practical decision-making.
