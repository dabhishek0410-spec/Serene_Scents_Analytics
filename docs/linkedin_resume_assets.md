# LinkedIn and Resume Assets

## LinkedIn Showcase Post

I built an end-to-end business analytics and forecasting project for Serene Scents, an early-stage handmade scented candle venture I worked with from July 2025 to January 2026.

The project analyzes daily units, revenue, cost, profit, seasonality, Diwali-driven demand, procurement cost improvements, and operational risk. I first built a basic forecasting model, then upgraded it into a more realistic business intelligence model with seasonality, festival boosts, volatility, delivery disruption logic, and forecast performance tracking.

Key results:

- Analyzed INR 19.97 lakh revenue and INR 8.34 lakh profit across 10,240 units sold.
- Identified margin improvement from 33.5% to 43.5% after procurement cost optimization.
- Found festival-window daily revenue was 54.2% higher than non-festival periods.
- Built actual-vs-forecast diagnostics showing why advanced scenario models need monthly calibration.
- Created portfolio-ready dashboards, KPI tables, SQL-ready logic, and founder decision insights.

This project helped me connect Python analytics with real business decisions: inventory planning, procurement timing, pricing discipline, seasonal readiness, and forecast governance.

## Resume Bullets

- Built an end-to-end Python analytics project for an early-stage candle venture, analyzing 10,240 units sold and INR 19.97 lakh revenue across July 2025 to January 2026.
- Developed basic and advanced forecasting models incorporating unit economics, seasonality, Diwali demand boosts, capacity constraints, volatility, and procurement delay scenarios.
- Identified procurement-led margin improvement from 33.5% to 43.5% after a 15% modeled unit cost reduction while maintaining a fixed INR 195 selling price.
- Created actual-vs-forecast diagnostics using MAE, RMSE, MAPE, and revenue bias to evaluate model performance and recommend monthly recalibration.
- Designed executive-ready KPI dashboards and SQL/Power BI reporting logic for revenue growth, profit margin, seasonal demand, forecast variance, and operational bottlenecks.

## Interview Talking Points

- I treated the forecast as a decision-support tool, not only a prediction exercise.
- The basic model performed better on aggregate revenue bias, but the advanced model surfaced more operational risks.
- I highlighted model limitations openly, including over-forecasting and the need for calibration.
- The strongest business insight was that margin improvement came from procurement discipline, not a price increase.
- Festival demand created revenue opportunity, but it also required inventory and supplier readiness.
