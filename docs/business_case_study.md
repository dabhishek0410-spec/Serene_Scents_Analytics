# Business Case Study Narrative

## Introduction

Serene Scents operated in the handmade scented candle and home fragrance category. The product sat at the intersection of decor, wellness, gifting, and festive consumption. As a small venture, the business had attractive unit economics but faced a familiar startup challenge: demand was growing faster than operational systems could mature.

From July 2025 to January 2026, the business needed a practical analytics model that could explain revenue growth, margin progression, demand spikes, and scaling pressure. The model also needed to be usable by a founder, not only by a technical analyst.

## Business Objective

The objective was to build a forecasting and business intelligence framework that could support:

- Revenue and profit planning
- Production capacity planning
- Raw material procurement timing
- Festival inventory preparation
- Pricing and margin decisions
- Monthly actual-vs-forecast review

## Startup Stage Challenges

Serene Scents faced typical early-stage operating constraints:

- Handmade production limited daily output.
- Raw material procurement was inconsistent.
- Demand changed around festivals, weekends, and gifting periods.
- A single price point had to cover cost volatility.
- Scaling introduced pressure on packaging, fulfillment, labor, and inventory.
- Demand signals were noisy because the business had limited historical data.

These challenges made simple forecasting insufficient. The business needed a model that could explain why revenue moved, not only predict how much revenue might occur.

## Scaling Journey

### July to September 2025: Small-Batch Baseline

The business began with low daily volume. July revenue was INR 65,130, and the business operated at a 33.5% gross margin. Early growth was visible, but the business was still proving the relationship between daily units, price, cost, and profit.

### October to November 2025: Operational Step-Change

October marked the most important inflection point. Revenue grew 149.2% month over month, supported by the Diwali demand window and better cost economics. The modeled unit cost reduction lifted margins to 43.5%, creating a stronger profit engine during the highest-demand period.

### December 2025: Peak Demand Capture

December produced the strongest month with INR 505,830 revenue. This validated the opportunity in home fragrance and giftable products, but it also showed the importance of maintaining procurement readiness and production capacity during peak periods.

### January 2026: Post-Season Normalization

January revenue declined 34.1% from December. This was a useful business signal: the founder needed a baseline forecast separate from seasonal uplift. Without this distinction, the business could overproduce, overbuy raw materials, or overestimate cash flow.

## Key Insights

### Operational Inefficiency Affected Profitability

The cost structure had a direct impact on profit. Before cost optimization, every candle sold at INR 195 generated about INR 65.30 profit. After the modeled 15% cost reduction, profit per unit increased to about INR 84.75. At scale, this difference became material.

### Scaling Introduced Supply Chain Pressure

As daily demand increased, procurement became a constraint. The stress-test model simulated 8 delivery delay days and INR 37,635 in revenue at risk. For a small business, this kind of disruption can affect customer satisfaction, cash flow, and repeat purchase behavior.

### Festival Demand Changed the Revenue Pattern

Festival-window average daily revenue was 54.2% higher than normal periods. This changed the operating question from "Will demand grow?" to "Can production and inventory be ready before demand arrives?"

### Volatility Modeling Improved Realism

The advanced model added random demand movement, festival boosts, and delivery disruption logic. Even though it overestimated actual revenue overall, it improved the model's usefulness as a planning framework because it exposed capacity risk, supplier risk, and seasonal sensitivity.

### Forecast Accuracy Needed Governance

The basic forecast had a -1.5% total revenue bias, while the advanced scenario had a +50.2% revenue bias. This does not mean the basic model was always better. It means the advanced model should be governed as a scenario model and recalibrated with actual results every month.

## Founder Decisions Enabled

- Build safety stock before Diwali and December gifting periods.
- Track supplier lead times and reorder points for wax, fragrance oils, jars, labels, and packaging.
- Use weekend staffing and production planning because weekend demand was materially higher.
- Maintain INR 195 pricing while using procurement savings to expand margin.
- Separate baseline demand, festival uplift, and stretch capacity scenarios in planning.
- Use actual-vs-forecast dashboards to identify overproduction or understocking risk.

## Conclusion

The Serene Scents project shows how analytics can help an early-stage business mature. The final output is not just a forecast model. It is a decision-support system for revenue planning, margin control, procurement discipline, and seasonal operating readiness.
